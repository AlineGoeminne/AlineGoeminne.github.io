Aline

Goeminne

I am currently an F.R.S.-FNRS postdoctoral researcher. I am supervised by Thomas Brihaye in the Department of Mathematics of the University of Mons (UMONS).

From October 2021 to September 2022, I was a CNRS postdoctoral researcher at Irisa, Rennes (France), in the SUMO team. I worked with Nicolas Markey and Ocan Sankur on Timed Network Congestion Games.
During my PhD thesis (from October 2017 to April 2021), I was co-supervised by Thomas Brihaye in the Department of Mathematics of the University of Mons (UMONS) and Jean-François Raskin in the Computer Science Department of the Université libre de Bruxelles (ULB).

My research interests lie in the fields of Game Theory and Formal Verification. In particular, I am interested in:

  • (multiplayer) games played on graphs
  • (multiplayer) timed games
  • applications of game theory, e.g., in logistics
